CRISP-ML lifecycle

Monitoring and Maintenance

Monitoring and maintenance ensure that SmartRecycleAI recommendations remain reliable as recycling infrastructure, population dynamics, waste generation patterns, and environmental risk variables evolve.

Lifecycle control

Keeping recommendations reliable over time

Continuous Supervision

Production models should be monitored regularly for prediction stability, class distribution shifts, data freshness, missing values, feature range changes, and unexpected recommendation patterns. Supervision helps detect problems before they affect planning decisions.

Model Updating

SmartRecycleAI should be retrained or recalibrated when new recycling center records, census updates, population projections, hazardous waste reports, or collection coverage data become available. Updates must preserve version control and reproducibility.

Degradation Detection

Data drift occurs when input data changes, such as new facility capacity or population growth. Concept drift occurs when the relationship between indicators and recommendation priority changes. Operational monitoring should track data drift and concept drift continuously because both can reduce model quality and must trigger review.

Risk Review

Ongoing risk review evaluates vulnerabilities, data quality controls, contextual changes, policy updates, and potential bias. Recommendations should remain explainable, auditable, and aligned with field evidence.

Maintenance signals

What SmartRecycleAI should watch

Recycling Infrastructure

Facilities can open, close, change status, or report different operating capacity.

Maintenance action: refresh facility registry features.

Population Dynamics

Population and household projections can change service demand and access pressure.

Maintenance action: update demographic projection features.

Waste Patterns

Waste generation, recyclable potential, and hazardous waste reports can evolve over time.

Maintenance action: monitor environmental pressure drift.

Monitoring plan

Drift, errors, and retraining triggers

Monitoring is required because Machine Learning systems degrade when the world changes. Recycling facilities may close, population pressure may increase, reporting patterns may change, and the model's original assumptions may become less valid.

Monitoring area Signal to review Maintenance response
Data drift Feature ranges, missing values, class distribution, new facility counts, and updated projections. Refresh the dataset, regenerate features, and compare distributions with previous reports.
Performance drift Lower accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, or expert disagreement with predictions. Re-evaluate the models on updated labels and retrain when performance falls below the accepted threshold.
Error tracking Invalid form submissions, missing artifacts, broken chart paths, or failed page loads. Log incidents, fix artifacts, and verify Flask routes before redeployment.
Governance Changes in policy, budget, recycling rules, or institutional priorities. Review model assumptions and document each new version before public use.

Maintenance Schedule

A practical academic schedule is monthly route and artifact validation, quarterly data refresh review, semester-level model retraining, and annual methodology review with updated environmental and municipal planning criteria.

Future Improvements

Future work should add automated monitoring reports, external validation labels, user feedback capture, model version comparison, cost-aware recommendations, GIS integration, and deployment logs.
